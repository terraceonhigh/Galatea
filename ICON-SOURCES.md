# Icon sources

Public-domain image archives that have proven useful as raw material for project icons. All three reward selective cropping — the icon hides inside a larger plate or photograph, and the work is finding it.

## Sources

- **Public Domain Image Archive** — <https://pdimagearchive.org/images/0b96d3ac-8cf3-4ffd-865f-f906a2fd7962/>
  Curated public-domain plates. Crop tight.

- **USDA National Agricultural Library — Pomological Watercolor Collection** — <https://search.nal.usda.gov/discovery/collectionDiscovery?vid=01NAL_INST:MAIN&inst=01NAL_INST&collectionId=81279629860007426>
  Early-20th-century watercolours of fruit cultivars. A single fruit, leaf, or pip is often the whole icon.

- **rh2ox on Flickr — botanical / heraldic plates** — <https://www.flickr.com/photos/rh2ox/albums/72157629191355220/>
  Scanned engravings and decorated initials. Good for monograms and heraldic shapes.

## Practice

Pick something with a strong silhouette at 64×64 px. If you can't read it at favicon size, it isn't the icon. Crop in a raster editor, then trace or threshold to the final form the project needs (SVG, ICNS, PNG set).

Provenance belongs in the project's own asset README, not stripped on the way in.
